Our Mission

To make cities healthier, more resilient, and more self-sufficient — by transforming vacant urban space into transparent, high-tech farms that grow fresh food close to home.

WHY WE EXIST

Two broken systems.
One fix.

Today's food system is too long, too fragile, and too disconnected from the communities it serves. Fresh produce often travels for days before it reaches a plate. Nutrient quality declines after harvest. Urban communities remain dependent on supply chains they cannot see.

At the same time, downtown Chicago has the highest office vacancy on record. Whole floors of beautiful buildings sit empty, searching for new ways to bring life and purpose back into their walls.

FARMZER0 was built to fix both at once — turn empty floors into farms, and the long supply chain into a 14-floor elevator ride.

The result is a new model where fresh food, public health, real estate revitalization, and local economic development all reinforce each other. One building. One block. One city at a time.

FOOD IS MEDICINE

Food should be part of the
healthcare conversation.

We believe access to fresh, nutrient-dense food can play a meaningful role in prevention, wellness, and long-term health. Our work is aligned with the growing Food Is Medicine and Food Rx movement — making fresh greens easier to access in the places where people already live, work, heal, and gather.

FARMZER0 partners with employers, healthcare systems, payers, food service operators, and civic institutions to bring transparent, hyper-local produce inside the buildings that need it most.

JULY 2024

First-of-its-kind concept vertical farm launched.

Our pilot farm went operational on the 18th floor of 30 N. LaSalle St., producing weekly harvests of premium greens and proving the office-floor-as-farm model in a live downtown tower.

FARM01 · 30 N. LASALLE 18TH FLOOR WEEKLY HARVESTS
NOVEMBER 2024

City of Chicago zoning change permits urban farming.

Indoor urban farming was officially permitted across all downtown DX and DC zoning districts — applicable to office and residential buildings in the central area. The most critical barrier for FARMZER0 to overcome, and the moment a true path to scalability and viability opened up.
